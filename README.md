This is currently a working project where my working draft is currently being written with the goal of getting it published. 

From December 2025 - May 2026, I worked as an Undergradute Researcher at UNC Charlotte under the supervision of Dr. Cori Faklaris where I was a part of the Security 
Privacy and Experiences (SPEX) Research Group and the Human Centered Computing Research Group (HCCRG). My project was to conduct an independent and systematic literature
review to understand why SMiShing is still a constant problem in the United States ... the Federal Trade Commission in 2024 estimated $470 million USD was lost due to 
smishing. 

**My research was guided by the following questions**
1. How are modern smishing campaigns structured and coordinated across the digital infrastructure?
2. What are the psychological and demographic factors that influence a user's susceptibility to smishing attacks?
3. How effective are current machine learning and technical detection systems in mitigating smishing?
4. What gaps exist in current smishing defenses, and what interdisciplinary strategies are needed to address them?

Upon my research, I focused on 22 different academic sources which varied from around the world. Smishing isn't going away in the United States due to many issues, 
including lack of governemnt support, lack of security education and training, lack of human centered computing designs, a lack of understanding of the smishing topic, and a lack of interdisciplinary research. Smishing is not just a technical issue, it's primary weapon is using human psychology.
